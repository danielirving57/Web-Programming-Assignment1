The Flash

The Flash follows Barry Allen, a forensic scientist who gains super-speed after a particle-accelerator explosion. Using his new abilities, he becomes the hero known as The Flash, fighting metahuman threats while trying to protect his loved ones and uncover the truth behind his mother’s murder. The series blends action, science-fiction, and emotional character arcs as Barry grows into one of the world’s greatest heroes.

Report on a Favourite AI Business Idea from “AI Businesses of 2025”

My favourite idea from the list is AI for fraud detection and prevention in banking because it directly protects people’s money and builds trust in financial systems. In today’s world, many people rely on online banking, card payments, and mobile apps to manage their finances. This makes banks a major target for criminals who use scams, fake websites, and stolen card details to steal money. AI can be applied in my world by being built into banking systems to watch transactions as they happen. For example, if someone usually spends small amounts in their local area and suddenly a large payment is made in another country, the system can notice this unusual behaviour and block the transaction or alert the bank and customer. This is useful because it can stop fraud before the money is lost, reduce stress for customers, and save banks large sums of money that would otherwise be lost to crime.

AI-based fraud detection is more effective than older rule-based systems because it can learn over time. As scammers change their methods, the AI system can study new patterns and improve how it identifies risky activity. This makes banking safer and helps banks respond faster to threats. It also supports customer confidence, since people feel more secure knowing that their bank is actively monitoring for fraud in the background.

However, there are important ethical issues related to this idea. One major concern is privacy, because the AI must look at transaction data to work properly. Customers may worry about how their information is used and stored. Another issue is fairness, since an AI system might wrongly flag certain people as suspicious based on incomplete or biased data. There is also the risk of over-reliance on automation, where human judgment is reduced and mistakes go unnoticed.

To address these moral concerns, banks should ensure that AI systems are transparent and only use customer data for fraud prevention purposes. Strong data protection laws and secure storage systems should be in place to protect personal information. AI decisions should also be reviewed by human staff, especially when an account is blocked or a transaction is declined. This keeps the system fair and prevents customers from being treated unjustly. Regular testing of the AI system can also help reduce bias and improve accuracy.

In conclusion, AI for fraud detection and prevention is a powerful and useful idea because it protects money, improves trust in banks, and adapts to new types of crime. When used responsibly with strong ethical rules, it can make financial systems safer for everyone.
